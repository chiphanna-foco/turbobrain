# Repairs | Ziprent Wiki

## Repairs: The "Superhero to the Rescue" Team

### What we do

Every property needs to be maintained to ensure it is habitable for the tenant. A well maintained property also attracts more prospects and possibly higher rent for the owners. Our system allows a one window solution to all maintenance needs for properties we manage. We continue to add specialist vendors in the areas we serve that offer the widest possible range of services so our tenants and owners can have a reliable resource to address all their maintenance needs that are within the scope of our PMA. We essentially serve as a bridge between owners, tenants and the specialized vendors. Our aim is to ensure all maintenance requests are received, processed, managed, completed and paid for in a timely manner — and that the work done by the vendor is of the highest quality.

### Workflow

#### Key steps

1. Tenant, Owner or Ziprent Representative Starts a Repair Request
2. Request enters Bidding Stage for Vendors to give us their bids or for their existing rates to be sent to the owner for approval
3. Owner Approves the bids presented or lets us know they will be handling themselves or with an External Vendor
4. Once Approved moves into scheduling phase to be scheduled with the Tenant and Vendor
5. Once scheduled date passes, Tenant and Vendor confirm they have completed the work and submit an invoice
6. The job is marked as Completed and the Invoice is processed

### Goal/Objective

**Department Goals:**
- Ensure quick turnaround time on all repairs
- Ensure any human induced delays are minimized not just internally but also by any stakeholders (Owners, Vendors and Tenants)
- Ensure reliable communication (quick response time on emails and minimum missed calls)
- Resolve bottlenecks, conflicts and any hindrances leading to delays in repairs

**Staff Expectations:**
- Be checked into phones for the duration of their shift (with the exception of breaks)
- Respond to a minimum of 65 emails per shift and maintain consistent pace
- Keep an eye out for communication gaps, delays and bottlenecks in line with department goals and make timely and key judgment calls to help speed up repairs and reduce overhead for all parties involved
- Be able to successfully manage an active job at any stage
- Properly process invoices and payments
- Properly set all expectations and communicate consistently

### Follow Ups

Naturally with hundreds of repairs every month, human delays are inevitable from stakeholders involved (tenants, owners and vendors). We, as the property manager have to be cognizant of such delays and have developed a standard follow up procedure with respective stakeholders to ensure everything is completed in a timely manner.

**General order of follow ups:**
Automated Email → SMS → Phone Call → Email

This is not to be an endless cycle or we will inundate the queue with stagnant repair threads. If the repair thread is not responded to by the concerned party at the time, we have to make an executive decision to somehow force progress or cancel the repair altogether.

**Key considerations in this process:**
- If the owner hasn't approved a repair regarding a hindrance, we will simply cancel it as this repair is not binding per the Lease Agreement
- If the owner hasn't approved a habitability issue in 72 hours, we will approve a repair with an estimate of up to $500 or below. If the repair bill is higher, we will reach out to a department manager for review and approval
- For urgent issues and emergencies, we expect our staff to make best judgment on skipping the default procedure to make a call first instead of an SMS or email in order to expedite the process
- Follow ups need to be succinct and to the point, but very specific to what action is necessary next

### Making a Repairs Call

Before making a Follow Up call for Repairs you have the unique advantage of time to research. Before making a call, click on the right hand side of Front App Contact to see if there are any other threads currently active for the contact you are calling. When you call them, you are being their representative for all of Ziprent on that call. BE PREPARED. Having those threads available in front of you will give you the opportunity to get not only move your thread forward but also to move forward or even close other existing threads. Often times when you get a tenant, owner or vendor on the phone they may have other things that they'd like to cover with you. Some will be completely out of your wheelhouse, but you are responsible to do your due diligence to have knowledge and some resolution to those other threads before and while you are on the call. You can then follow up on appropriate threads with resolutions or other questions they brought up on the call. This is INCREDIBLY helpful when you have a vendor on the phone and can push forward multiple threads at once and snooze appropriately.

### Receiving Repairs Calls

#### Receiving Tenant Call

Tenants are usually looking for an update on a repair. Please search for the repair thread and let them know what step it is in and explain the process:

**Bidding:** You will receive an email confirmation that your repair request has been submitted. At this time the Repair Team gathers information and photos, if not already present. Vendors will generally require photos. Bidding might take 48 hours or it could be within minutes if the category of job has set minimums.

**Approving:** You will receive an email notifying you that we have finished collecting bids and have sent the Vendor information and bids to the owner for approval.

**Scheduling:** You will receive an email informing you that the job has been approved and is in the process of scheduling without a confirmed visit date/time. Under most circumstances the vendor will reach out to you, but, To expedite the process, please feel free to reach out to them.

**Confirmed:** You will receive an email stating that a date/time for the repair visit has been confirmed.

**Completed:** When an invoice has been received on our end this email is sent. If the tenant is following up on scheduling or confirmation please use the HOW TO TASK section below to initiate then next steps and send a full follow up email and link to the phone call.

#### Receiving Owner Call

Owners most often call to understand the process of the repairs process here are the steps:

**Bidding:** We'll ensure descriptions and photos are included and the repair legitimate and if so how urgent. Bidding might take 48 hours or it could be within minutes if the category of job has set minimums.

**Approving:** Vendor information and bids are sent for review and approval.

**Scheduling:** Job has been approved and is in the process of scheduling without a confirmed time.

**Confirmed:** Time is confirmed.

**Completed:** Invoice received. The majority of job categories will have photos included. We file the invoice with your property, and your associated account will be debited and a receipt emailed. You don't have to do anything at this point in time.

If an owner is approving please click approve in the email thread for the repairs job in Front. Then send follow up recap of the call on that thread as well and link to the phone call.

#### Receiving Vendor Call

A vendor call will either follow up for scheduling or likely regarding the payment on an invoice. First search in Front if we have received the invoice search by work number or property address. We also need pictures of the completed job before we can process. Often we need to remind the vendor of this even if they've been emailed that we need that follow up. If a vendor is having access issues please assist immediately, they should have tenant information and lockbox information if there was lockbox access. If needed you can create a new code or resend code to the vendor. Then send a follow up email from the appropriate thread and link to the phone call.

### Workflow Overview

#### Inbox Overview

**Repairs: Start:** Any email sent to repairs@ziprent.com will land in this inbox. Owners and tenants previously could not submit repair requests through their portal had to send us an email for staff to create the repair task in the Dashboard. This naturally was inefficient as we were playing relay and had to do a lot of back and forth with tenants/owners to gather enough evidence in order to start a meaningful repair task that vendors could bid on. In early 2023, we added the feature so owners and tenants can themselves submit a repair using evidence they have in the form of pictures or videos. After this, repairs start has an auto-responder guiding anyone sending email to submit repairs online. Furthermore, our phone threads for repairs department land in this inbox.

**Repairs: Active:** All jobs that been entered into the Ziprent platform are actively being worked on in this inbox through dedicated threads.

**Repairs: Invoice:** All jobs that have been completed and only need payment processed are in this inbox.

**Repairs: Closed:** All repairs that have been completed are automatically moved to this queue. The completed repair thread will still be in 'open' status and will have to be manually archived. Often stakeholders can continue to respond to closed repairs with unrelated queries, we want to separate these in the closed repair queues so active repairs aren't delayed.

**Repairs: Bestbuy:** We used to use Bestbuy exclusively for new appliances back in the day and had a dedicated queue as well as an account manager at Bestbuy who would deal with Ziprent orders. We now work with Homedepot as well. Communication with both Bestbuy and The Home Depot is largely handled through this inbox.

#### Tag Strategy

Tags are used for 2 reasons:
- For organization and for quickly communicating specifics about a job without having to read through all thread history

**Job Status Tags:** Green and Blue tags that quickly communicate the active or closed status of a job — i.e. bidding, approving, completed, and canceled tags (amongst others)

**Common Tags:** Grey tags seen on every job thread that quickly communicate the basics of a job and are used for organization — i.e. category and address tags (amongst others)

**Dependent Detail Tags:** Colored tags that quickly communicate any detail that falls outside of the normal repairs process which successful completion of the given job depends on — i.e. urgent, priority, access, and payment tags (amongst others)

#### Urgent vs. Priority

**Urgent Repairs:**
- Habitability issues (important and time sensitive repairs)
- Time critical repairs (not important but time sensitive repairs)

**Priority Repairs:**
- Escalated repairs (where a party is frustrated and is complaining about our services or threatening to leave a bad review for any reason)
- Reno/Rehab repairs (repairs that are outside the scope of our services that need to be monitored)
- Sensitive repairs (where one or more parties are sensitive and need special attention than regular repairs)

13. If incoming maintenance request required more than 1 job to be started:

### Repairs: Active Inbox

#### Bidding (green Bidding Tag)

**Enter Bids and Send for Approval (if bids received from Vendor):**
1. Open Manage Bid module
2. Edit vendor (hamburger button next to vendor at bottom of Manage Bid module)
3. Enter bid in Notes section and price in Price Quote section
4. Click Store only (not Store + Request Bid)
5. Repeat for all bids received (if more than one received)

**Request Owner approval (once all bid/s entered and stored):**
1. Click Email Owner button at the bottom of the same module
2. Ensure automated bid approval emails sent in the job thread
3. Snooze thread for 48 hours

**Followup for Bids from Vendors (if bids not yet received):**
1. Check job thread for the automated emails to vendors
2. Follow up for a bid via email to the vendors that were sent automated bid request

#### Approving (green Approving Tag)

**Approve Vendor (if approval received):**
1. Approval of a bid *must* be received from the property owner
2. Navigate to the property page in the Ziprent platform
3. Match the job # in the thread to the open job in the platform under the property page
4. Click the hamburger button on the proper open job and click Schedule
5. Select the vendor the owner approved from the dropdown (leave the to/from date/times blank)
6. Click Store
7. Ensure automated scheduling emails sent in the job thread
8. Snooze thread for 72 hours

**Followup for Approval (if approval not yet received):**
1. Copy bid(s) from the previous automated email from Ziprent to the owner requesting bid approval
2. Email the property owner for approval to move forward with a bid and paste the bid option(s) within the body of the followup email
3. Snooze thread for 48 hours

#### Scheduling (green Scheduling Tag)

**Schedule Appointment (if appointment details received):**
1. Appointments may be scheduled upon receipt of dates/times from any coordinating party
2. Navigate to the property page in the Ziprent platform
3. Match the job # in the thread to the open job in the platform under the property page
4. Click the hamburger button on the proper open job and click Schedule
5. Ensure the correct vendor is selected, enter the proper date/times in the to/from boxes
6. Click Store
7. Ensure the automated confirmed emails sent in the job thread
8. Snooze thread until the morning after the appointment using Day & Time

**Follow-up for Appointment Details (if not yet received):**
1. Check the automated scheduling emails for the selected property access
2. Determine which parties need to schedule together
3. Send email to proper pair to inquire if an appointment has been scheduled
4. Followup with both parties for appointment details for a quicker response - Or followup with only the vendor if lockbox access
5. Snooze thread for 72 hours

#### Confirmed (green Confirmed Tag)

**Followup on Completion of Schedule appointment:**
1. Email vendor to confirm job was completed as scheduled
2. Email tenant to confirm job was completed as scheduled
3. If access is lockbox, only follow up with vendor
4. Snooze thread for 48 hours

**Followup for Invoice:**
1. If confirmation of job completion already received
2. Email vendor for invoice
3. Change green tag from Confirmed Tag to Invoice Tag
4. Snooze thread for 48 hours

### Repairs: Invoice Inbox

#### Match Invoice to Proper Job

1. Check address on invoice
2. Tag thread with the proper address tag and pin to workspace
3. Match invoice to job thread under address (thread may be open or snoozed)

#### Process Payment

1. Save copy of the invoice to your computer
2. Complete Repair - Navigate to the property page in the Ziprent platform
3. Match the job # in the thread to the open job in the platform under the property page
4. Click the hamburger button on the proper open job and click Completed
5. Ensure proper vendor is selected in dropdown
6. Enter $ amount from matched invoice
7. Enter completed job description (copied from invoice)
8. Attach invoice (and completed job photos if received)
9. Click Store
10. Add Invoice - Invoice module will automatically open after storing Completed module
11. Ensure information in all fields is correct
12. Select ACH as payment method
13. Ensure Charge Owner box is checked
14. Click Add Invoice
15. Ensure automated completed job emails sent in job thread
16. Archive completed job thread

If ACH payment method not available:
1. Close Invoice module (do not click Add Invoice)
2. Return to job thread
3. Ensure automated completed job emails sent in job thread
4. Send vendor canned response titled "Vendor ACH"
5. Snooze for 7 days

**Followup for Invoice:**
1. Email vendor for Invoice for confirmed completed job
2. Snooze thread for 7 days

### Policy

#### Lightbulbs, Filters, Batteries

The purchase and installation of lightbulbs, batteries and filters (fridge, water, stove, not HVAC) are the owner's responsibility at the start of the lease, and the tenant's responsibility to maintain during. Detectors are the responsibility of the owner for the duration of the lease, and the tenants responsibility to notify management immediately if inoperable.

#### Bedbugs

Bedbugs are habitability risk and needs to be handled as soon as possible. As the landlord to fix the issue and make the property habitable again, regardless of whether the tenant agrees to pay for this work. When the tenant exits, any issues where there is proof that the issues were caused by the tenant's abuse or negligence, it would then be deducted from the security deposit if they do not agree to pay for the service at that time.

#### Failures Caused by Tenants

Repairs that are found to be caused by tenant's misuse will be the tenant's responsibility to pay the cost of the repair. Owners are held responsible to pay for repairs that are only caused by normal use.

#### Home Warranty

The reason why Ziprent wouldn't be able to deal directly with the home warranty is because the home warranty usually ask us questions that we wouldn't know (account info, personal info, etc.) therefore we ask owners to initiate the request themselves and we can help with scheduling once a vendor is assigned. If Ziprent isn't able to deal directly with the home warranty, there is a very high likelihood that the tenants wouldn't be able to work with them directly either.
