# Escalations

How to handle bad reviews / escalated issues

## Escalated Issues

It is inevitable that a bad review or escalated issue will surface. Seeing as our reputation is one of the cornerstone for our brand and identity, it is absolutely paramount that we take the necessary steps to resolve the issue as quickly as possible.

Please make sure you reference and follow the below steps:

### Initial Response Process

1. When a bad review first comes in, we should aim to try and give the person a call
2. Often times, the person just want their issue solved
3. At that point, we want to let them vent and be there to ensure that we will work to resolve the issue as quickly as possible
4. Whether you spoke to the person or not, we want bring the conversation to the escalations inbox
5. Start a fresh thread in the escalations inbox recapping what you spoke about (if applicable) and outlining "management" will be looking into the situation
6. This gives some reassurance to the individual that it is one our radar and buys us time to review the situation in more detail and come up with a path to resolution
7. Link all of the relevant email threads in the comments so we as a team can familiarize ourselves quickly

### Maintaining Leverage

Whenever we are trying to get a review removed or updated, we always want to make sure we maintain leverage. We should always make sure to resolve the issue first. Once the issue is resolved, we can then figure out what kind of compensation or action we can take that will incentivize that person to remove their review.

Often times, people will share the following sentiment, "You're only reaching out to me because I left a bad review….". That is correct, but what other company does so? We are there to listen and learn from our mistakes. Showing humility and accepting responsibility goes a long way in rebuilding the trust we lost initially.

## "How Did We Do?" Emails

The "How did we do?" emails are labeled in the subject line with the property address of the owner or tenant in the Escalations inbox. All tenants and owners are sent this email asking for their experience and feedback.

### Negative Responses

If the owner or tenant responds negatively, we want to address their experience/feedback and resolve any outstanding issues.

### Positive Responses

If the owner or tenant responds positively, we want to follow the below steps:

1. BCC staff@ziprent.com
2. Use canned response "HDWD: Owner Response" or "HDWD: Tenant Response"
3. In order to determine if the email to us was sent by the owner or tenant, you will want to read the automated email that was sent to them
4. In the last sentence of the automated email, if it says "customers", this is an owner email. If it says "renters", this is a tenant email
5. Send the email and archive the thread

### Follow-Up for Review Links

If the owner or tenant responds to the email (asking for links for reviews):

1. Send the canned response "HDWD: Have Yelp?" to prompt them to answer if they are an active Yelp user
2. Once they respond, use the canned response "HDWD/CSAT: Links"
3. If their email confirms that they are an active Yelp user, include a Yelp link
4. If their email confirms that they are not an active Yelp user, do not send them a Yelp link
5. In the canned response, there is guidance on what links to send (priority set as listed from top to bottom)
6. Information on how often you want to spread out reviews for all platforms (specifically for Yelp and Google)

### Link Management

1. Please make sure you remove all unnecessary info to the customer when sending the email (remove the area notes next to Yelp and Google, and remove the number of reviews and ratings, as this is all internal information)
2. All responses should include a Google, BBB, and Zillow link
3. Trustpilot will send a link automatically to the customer once we add their email to BCC
4. Please be sure to copy and paste the Yelp or Google link into the Escalations & Escalations: CSAT inbox prior to sending it to the customer to see when the link was last sent to our customers
5. We do not want to send the same link to two customers in a short time frame within one another to avoid two reviews coming in on the same page(s) simultaneously, especially for Yelp pages
6. If a Yelp link was sent to a customer, it should not be sent again to any customer until 3-4 weeks have gone by
7. Once the links have been sent to them, you may archive the thread

### Review Confirmation

1. Once the customer responds confirming that they've submitted at least one review on any of the links (one of them must be on Yelp or Google), please review and confirm that they did leave a review
2. For Yelp and Google, their reviews should be seen almost immediately
3. Zillow, BBB, and Trustpilot may take some time for their review to be seen by the public
4. As long as they left a review for Yelp or Google, they are good to receive the $100 credit

### Credit Processing

1. Please respond to the review publicly
2. Send the canned response "HDWD: Credit" once you've went in Ziprent Admin and sent them a $100 credit
3. Go to Accounts Payable, Add Accounts Payable, ACH option
4. Category: Brand Feedback
5. Fill out the appropriate entity, property, and date
6. You may then archive the thread

## CSAT Inbox

CSAT stands for customer satisfaction score. Emails come into CSAT inbox when customers rate our services.

### How Customers Rate Services

There are two ways they rate our services:

**First Way** — The first and most common way customers rate our services is when an action has been completed (lease signed, repair completed, etc). They are asked to rate our services at the bottom of the completed emails for a chance to win a gift card. When they rate our services this way, an email will come in with the subject line "New Review".

**Second Way** — When they use the 5 stars below any email to rate their communication with our team. When they rate our services this way, an email will come in with a forwarded message of the email they had rated. You will want to take the email address under "to" section, search up the email (find the related thread), and look for the comment that shows how the customer rated our services. Once you've found the Customer Satisfaction Survey comment, you will want to screenshot this comment and place it in the comments section of the CSAT thread.

### Handling Negative CSAT Responses

If the customer responds negatively, we want to address their experience/feedback and resolve any outstanding issues.

### Handling Positive CSAT Responses

Similar to the "How did we do?" emails, we treat CSAT similarly. The only difference is our initial response and last response regarding the credit amount.

If the owner or tenant responds positively, you will send one of the following CSAT canned responses:

- "CSAT Application Positive"
- "CSAT Manager/Listing Positive Review"
- "CSAT Repair Positive"

You may need to edit the response as needed to ensure it matches the comment or review with the appropriate department.

### Getting Tenants to Leave an Online Review

Then, you'll want to follow the steps of "How did we do?" emails to get them to leave an online review.

Once the online review has been left:

1. Check if the customer has an associated bank account with Ziprent
2. Go under Ziprent Admin, Accounts Payable, Add Accounts Payable
3. Search their email address under Vendor section and see if ACH is an option under Recipient payment method
4. If it is, you will follow step #6 in the "How did we do?" emails (send $100 credit via ACH)
5. If it is not an option, you will want to tag @tom in the comments so that Tom can send over a $50 gift card
6. Once the gift card has been sent, send the "CSAT: Credit" canned response over to the customer

## All Other Inquiries

Any inquiry that comes into Escalations must be treated with care. Many of the inquiries are from frustrated customers who want their issues resolved.

For any other inquiry:

1. Please always change the subject line to "Escalations | Property Address"
2. Confirm receipt of their email
3. Confirming receipt of their email buys you time to review their issue, address it, and work towards a resolution
4. Customers must feel heard and understood within Escalations to avoid them from going public with how they feel about our service
5. The #1 goal is to ensure customers that Escalations team is here to help and will find a resolution
6. The most common frustration point for our customers is the amount of people involved in their issue
7. Typically, customers prefer one person work on their issue to avoid miscommunication or confusion about the issue
8. Escalations is the place where they get this one-on-one interaction therefore the expectation is that you continue to work on their issue closely until it is resolved
