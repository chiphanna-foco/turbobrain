# Owner Insurance

Details on owner's insurance for their property and what is required

## Policy Requirements

Homeowners policies that meet Ziprent's PMA requirements must have the following (section 5E of the PMA):

### Coverage Requirements

**General Liability** — Coverage in an amount no less than $500,000 ($1,000,000 recommended).

**Additional Insured** — Policy must list Ziprent as a named Additional Insured or Second Named Insured

*The main thing to look out for is the word liability in the homeowner's insurance document and ensure that the amount is no less than $500k. Please note that owners may have different liability coverages within the policy that could have a combined total of $500k+ which does meet our requirements. It is not required for one specific liability type to have $500k+. This can include totaling the liability coverages in a primary policy plus an umbrella policy.*

### Condo Exception

If an owner replies their insurer will not go up to $500k, then $300k in liability coverage for condos is acceptable. Some insurers simply will not offer $500k for condos. Furthermore, having Ziprent listed as Additional Insured is always more important than the liability coverage amount. For example, we always prefer an owner to have $300k in liability coverage with Ziprent listed as Additional Insured over an owner having $500k in liability coverage without Ziprent listed.

## Management Services & Tenant Placement

Ziprent's requirement for homeowner insurance is applicable to all of our services. When Ziprent has any involvement in placing persons in a property, coverage is necessary. For this reason, homeowners insurance with Ziprent listed as Additional Insured is required for Tenant Placement and all other services not just ongoing management.

## Additional Insured vs Additional Interest

When it comes to insurance, an Additional Interest is an entity with a financial interest in the property that's being insured. An Additional Insured, on the other hand, is an entity that is also covered by the insurance policy purchased. This is why Ziprent must be listed as Additional Insured, not Additional Interest.

## Other Policy Coverages and Conditions

Other than the requirements noted above, Owners have full discretion in regards to any other coverages or amounts included in their policy

### Policy Verbiage

- **Additional Insured** — Ziprent is covered by the insurance policy
- **Additional Interest (coverage)** — While Additional Interest normally does not mean that Ziprent is covered, some companies do provide coverage if it says Additional Interest on the forms
- **Automatically Covered** — This means Ziprent is covered without specifically saying we are on the policy; the policy just covers us by default
- **Other Language that provides Ziprent coverage** — Secondary Insured, Additional Named Insured, Insured, Second Named Insured, Liability Extended to Others
- **Does not Support** — This means that the insurance company will not cover Ziprent in any way, Additional Interest can also fall under this description if Ziprent will not be covered by the policy, but only listed for notifications. THEY HAVE TO GET A NEW POLICY TO WORK WITH US

Obie does offer coverage for condos and multi-unit properties.

**Instructions from Obie:**

- If it's 4 units or less, they need to choose duplex through quadplex, not apartment building
- If it's over 4 units then they would be kicked into the multifamily user flow, which would have an agent reach out to them

Direct from Obie — There will be some location restrictions. High wildfire, barrier island, coastal properties, etc. In California we will usually find restrictions for properties within half a mile from the coast or in a high wildfire area.
