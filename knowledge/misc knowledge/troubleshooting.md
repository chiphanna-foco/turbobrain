# Troubleshooting

What to do when having issues, for staff and for owners/tenants

Below are basic steps to take once you have assisted someone but are not able to resolve their technical issue (using our website) or where they are seeing an error. Before escalating this to Management or Dev team please follow the steps below:

## Tenant/Owner/Vendor

If you can't solve the customer's issue while on the phone:
1. Create an email recapping the call and ask for a picture of the issues (if they are receiving an error)
2. Request details regarding where they are trying to access the information (adding a bank account, logging in, etc…)
3. Ensure the details of where they received this error or issue are outlined in your recap email

**Note:** This is NOT for LOCKBOX ISSUES

If you can resolve it, still recap the call but let them know they can reach back out if they need further assistance.

### Email Recap Questions

In the email recap from a phone call or response to their email, please send the following questions to them:
- What is the URL you have the issue at?
- What is the exact error you have received?
- Have you cleared your devices cache? (you can find details here and if not please do so: https://its.uiowa.edu/support/article/719)
- If it has been over an hour since you had the issue can you please try again?
- Please provide a screenshot or image of the issue

**Template:** Please use the email template from the manager inbox to send to them

### Final Step
Tag @noah or current internal support

## Staff (Internal Technical Issues)

### Phone Issues

Should you have issues with answering or making phone calls, please compile ALL the following information before sending the email (DO NOT SEND ANY OTHER WAY).

Copy the below lines to a new email and answer each question inline:

- Phone model (or if using your computer, desktop calling)
- What is your Cellular service?
- Is Wifi calling (this is a cellphone only setting) on or off? Try switching it off if you have it on or vice-a-versa and waiting for a call
- Reception level in the spot your phone is placed (or wifi signal if using PC to make calls) - rate on a scale of 0 bars to full bars
- If using a computer please run a speed test and include a screenshot of the results (need all the data): https://getvoip.com/speed-test-tool/
- Are you using a headset? Wireless or Wired?
- Do you move your phone around the room/desk when you answer the call?
- Is this only incoming calls with Ziprent or does it happen when making an outgoing call on your phone, directly from your phone number?
- Are you using any hotspot or cell booster with your device? If so, what is the make/model? If using a hotspot or cellbooster please turn it off
- Did you log out and log back in of your Ziprent account when it happens?
- Do you have any calls, outside of Ziprent calls, with that issue?
- Have you tried to have someone call your phone number (not from a Ziprent number) right after this issue? If not, please do so when it happens again
- Are you getting the email/text message for the incoming call?
- When you get the email/text of an incoming call, how long until your phone rings?
- Please make sure that the incoming Ziprent phone number is added as a contact for your phone
- Please make sure that the incoming Ziprent phone number is not on any blocklist app for your cell phone:
  - Apple: https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT207099
  - Android: https://support.google.com/phoneapp/answer/3459196?hl=en
- Please link at least 2 incoming and 1 outgoing phone calls that this issue has occurred with
- Please also fill out this form: https://forms.gle/EAkkCzgYPn1qQtC17

### Other Support Issues

Should you have other issues, follow these troubleshooting steps:

1. Reset your password
2. Login and logout of your account (google, front and ziprent.com)
3. Clear your cache - https://its.uiowa.edu/support/article/719
4. Try another browser on your computer
5. Try another device that is on your network (cellphone with wifi on)
6. Try another device to confirm it isn't your network (cellphone with wifi off)
7. Restart your computer or device

If the above does not work, write the exact steps that you need to take to recreate the error and also take a screenshot of the error you received.

### Information to Include in Support Email

Then please send that in an email, including the following questions and your answers to them:
- What device are you having a problem with (computer / phone)?
- What browser are you having a problem on?
- When did it start?
- Is it constantly happening?
- Is anyone else on Ziprent staff having the issue? If so, who?
- Did you do the following tasks?
  - Reset your password
  - Login and logout of your account (google, front and ziprent.com)
  - Clear your cache - https://its.uiowa.edu/support/article/719
  - Restart your computer or device

### Final Step
Email noah@ziprent.com or current internal support
