# Manager: The "Working Relationship" Team

## What we do

Manager department is the default help desk contact at Ziprent. It serves many key roles:

- **Bridge between owners and tenants** - as well as a gateway to other departments for tenants and homeowners. Any unrouted query not specific to any department will land in Manager. We try to handle the request to the best of our abilities and transfer to another department only when absolutely necessary.

- **First point of contact for prospective tenants** - Our success hinges upon finding as many qualified prospective tenants for home owners and it is the job of the Manager department to convert all possible leads and contacts from Showings and Listings queues into applicants for the owner's consideration. This can only happen through keen knowledge of the properties, attention to detail and ability to quickly address any and all concerns a prospective tenant may have.

- **Point of contact for HOA** - tied to properties we manage. Our job is to review the email, identify the relevant stakeholders to inform and then relay the request. This helps ensure tenants and owners are kept informed about maintenance, rule changes, violations among other things.

Owing to the diverse nature of issues landing in the Manager department, an experienced Manager staff possesses deep knowledge of all our services, has a good idea of inner workings of other departments, is adept at finding information quickly and is able to make key decision calls quickly. We want tenants to feel at home in the property and feel comfortable enough to reach out if there is an issue we need to know about. The Manager Team is the go-to for tenants who have questions about their lease, property, and other rental related issues. Our managers are experienced in clear communication to understand what the issue is and how we can help.

## FAQ's

### 1099's

At the start of the new year, until the end of January, we need to process 1099s for vendors and for owners. We do not need their W9 forms and they need to fill out their tax information in their dashboard. Here is the direct link: https://www.ziprent.com/auth/owner/tax-forms

General staff doesn't have access to their dashboard so the owner will need to do it themselves. (A supervisor actually can, if there is pushback, but it is far better for them to do it themselves as we won't have their SSN/TIN information)

If they have previously received a 1099 from us, it will be prefilled with the information, they just need to confirm it. Security deposits are excluded only from 1099s. That means it won't show up under the 1099 we send. We do not issue paper 1099s. They can print it via their dashboard if they need it.

- Vendor's 1099s are due by January 31st
- Owner's 1099s are due by March 31st (but we push everyone to get it to us by January 31st)

If an owner requests a tax form for a previous year that unfortunately is not possible. After the due date of the 1099 we can no longer generate 1099s for that year.

**Can owners split taxes?** Yes, we can split the 1099 equally between 2 owners only. This is completely handled by the system.

### Lease Break

**Q: What is the process for breaking a lease early? Is there a specific lease breaking fee?**

A: While tenants will be fully responsible for rent payments during the entire term of the lease, we understand that unforeseen circumstances may arise. In a case where the tenants must vacate the premises, should the tenants cooperate with showing the unit to prospective tenants, Ziprent, in concert with the owner, will locate, vet and sign a new lease for the premises at the current cost of tenant placement (or whatever amount is stated in your lease) and will be paid by the tenant. Tenants agree to pay for all rent up until the move-in day of the new lease and leave at least a 5 day gap between their departure and the new tenants' move-in in order to allow for potential work to be performed to the unit. Tenants are responsible for repaying the TP fee which was paid by the owner upon the tenant placement. Generally, we would request that you help show the property at least 3-4 times a week (or empty the unit so we can perform showings) and as soon as we find a viable replacement and the new tenants start paying rent, your lease would be canceled.

## Move In Walkthrough

Every tenant must complete a walkthrough and record the state of the property as well as highlight any issues they see. Our automated system notifies them and helps them through the process. Once they submit the walkthrough, automated emails are sent through Front Manager:Walkthrough Inbox. Our team has to review both inspector and tenant walkthroughs to identify habitability issues and ensure they are ironed out for safety and comfort of the tenants.

### Issue Types

Issues identified in a walkthrough by tenants or inspectors can be of three types:

**Habitability & Emergency:** California's implied warranty of habitability requires a landlord to keep the premises in a condition fit for the occupation of human beings.

Issues that would be considered habitability & Emergency issues:
- Issues with the HVAC system (heat or air conditioning not properly functioning)
- Issues with the plumbing in the home (leaks, water heater, non-functioning plumbing fixtures)
- Issues with any appliances in the home (not functioning properly)
- Issues with front door/main house keys & locks
- Issues with the Smoke/CO detectors inside the home
- Pests/Rodents/Mold etc (excluding ants)

While drain clogs are not considered habitability, they should be treated urgently.

**Hindrances:** These are issues identified by tenants (marked bad) but not critical towards habitability. The owner may or may not approve these. Examples could be a couple of bulbs out in a ceiling lamp, or scuff marks on the paint.

**Conditions:** In addition to these, sometimes, tenants will mark items as OK but will add remarks on their condition. Something like marking a door as working/OK but then "there are scratches". These are important for tenants to note as they may need to refer back to them at the end of the lease to claim security deposit.

### Detectors

Detectors number and placement in accordance with the following. New detectors should be powered by 10 year lithium ion battery.

- **CO** = floors | one on each floor including basements
- **Smoke** = floors + bedrooms | one on each floor and in each bedroom

There is usually not a detector in the kitchen as it would go off all the time. If there is a detector in the living room, dining room or hallway and it is a one story home there only needs to be ONE for that floor there does not need to be one in every room.
