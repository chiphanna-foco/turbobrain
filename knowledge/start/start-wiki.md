# Start Department | Ziprent Wiki

## Overview

**Start: The "Growing the Business" Team**

The Start Team helps expand Ziprent's reach and services nationwide. New leads are evaluated to determine if Ziprent services are right for them and their property. As the first impression for potential clients, the Start Department is essential to Ziprent's growth.

### Purpose
- Grow Ziprent with happy owners
- Thoroughly explain Ziprent services
- Set clear expectations to prevent surprises
- Convert leads to signed Property Management Agreements (PMA)

### Staff Expectations
- Checked into phones for minimum 6 hours per shift
- Respond to minimum 50 emails per shift
- No more than 3 missed calls
- Emails in inbox not exceeding 12 hours

## Lead Ownership Model

**Assignment & Ownership**
- Each lead assigned to a salesperson
- Salesperson responsible for lead through entire lifecycle
- Status tracked in Front (New → Warm → Hot → PMA Sent → Signed or Resolved)
- Ownership ends when PMA signed with complete handoff notes

**Sales Pipeline Stages**
- **OPEN**: Active thread requiring sales action
  - New: Initial status
  - Warm: Two-way conversation started
  - Hot: High probability of PMA
- **WAITING**: Owner not ready today but has clear timeline
- **PMA SENT**: Sent to owner; awaiting signature
- **RESOLVED**: Closed or inactive
  - Signed: PMA executed
  - Not a Fit: Property/owner doesn't qualify
  - Unresponsive: No response for 30+ days
  - Invalid/Duplicate: Spam or false submission

## Taking a Start Call

**Six Parts of a Consultative Call**

1. **Opening**: Greet, introduce yourself, thank them
2. **Set the Game Plan**: How the call will go, deliver value props
3. **Discovery**: Ask about their goals, property details, experience
4. **Proposal**: Explain why Ziprent fits their needs
5. **Close the Deal**: Handle objections, send PMA if ready
6. **Thank**: Recap and confirm next steps

## Key Objections & Rebuttals

**"Your fees are too high"**
- Competitors charge 4-12% of monthly rent for PM and 50-60% for TP
- Flat-fee model removes incentive for unnecessary work

**"You are not local"**
- Have trusted vendors in every market
- 70+ direct employees + vast vendor network
- Open 16 hours/day (vs. traditional 9-5)
- Posts to 30+ listing sites

**"I don't feel comfortable with unattended showings"**
- Conducted 100,000+ showings with minimal incidents
- Vacant properties are greatest risk regardless of showing method
- Same-day inspection capability if issues reported
- Property monitored throughout day (8am-8pm)

**"You won't help me get property move-in ready"**
- Services require no upfront payment
- Can assist with minor repairs (paint, cleaning, detectors) AFTER tenant placed
- Full repairs/renovations owner's responsibility before listing

**"Ziprent doesn't handle evictions"**
- 99.8% success rate in tenant placement (5,000+ tenants)
- Less than 0.2% of managed properties needed eviction
- Handle 3-day, 30-day, 60-day notices
- Refer to recommended attorneys
- Refund full TP fee if eviction needed

## How-To Guides

### Taking Notes for Start Calls
- Lead Info: Name, phone, email, address, property type
- Situation: Occupancy, timeline, condition
- Goals/Pain: What matters to owner
- Service: Type requested (PM/TP, etc.)
- Objections: How handled
- Next Step: Date/time/actions needed
- Source: Where lead came from

### Creating Market Analysis
1. Go to Rentometer.com or Ziprent dashboard
2. Enter property address and bed/bath count
3. Look back 90 days, search radius 1 mile
4. Select correct building type (House/Condo/Apt)
5. Generate report and send with template

### Sending PMA
1. Enter owner information into system
2. Generate PMA document
3. Send to owner for review and signature
4. Once signed, owner moves to Onboarding

## FAQ

**Agreement Details**
- Combined TP and PM agreement
- Month-to-month, no commitment
- PM fee only charged if collecting rent
- Can cancel anytime without penalties

**Out of State Owners**
- No income withholding (consult CPA)
- Ziprent facilitates transfers, doesn't hold funds
- Owner responsible for tax filing

**Property Must Be Move-In Ready**
- All repairs completed
- Professionally cleaned
- Paint touch-ups done
- Cannot coordinate large renovations

**Utilities**
- Multi-unit properties: recommend $50 water + $50 utilities per tenant
- Split equally, averages over time
- Owner/tenant responsible for their respective utilities

**Insurance**
- Ziprent requires to be listed as Additional Insured
- Standard for liability protection on both parties
- Doesn't give Ziprent financial interest
- Unified defense if litigation occurs
